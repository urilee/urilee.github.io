University of Michigan Studio

Leadership Studio - August 2017



How Can We:

Ensure that disabled are the only ones who have access to ADA seats on public transportation

The Problem:

Regular community members were sitting in ADA seats and occassionally refusing to get up for those who require those seats.

The Solution:

Our final idea aided users who required the ADA seats and have a state regulated pass that proves their disability. When getting on public transportation users are handed a large token, which is placed into a token machine which unfolds the ADA regulated chair, allowing the user to sit. The seat is initially covered at an angle to prevent others from sitting. When the user gets up to leave, the chair will extend back into it's position.
